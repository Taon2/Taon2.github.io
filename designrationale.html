Design Rationale

The design for this portfolio site has an interesting beginning.

Before joining my UX 1400 class, this website existed as a developer portfolio for potential employers to view. My profile picture on many websites is red, as seen in the top left corner, so I decided to stick to the theme of a red throughout the website. The pale background and dark contrasts help make that color pop and look appealing. I chose to expand upon my existing structure for my UX 1400 class, as I knew it could be used as an opportunity to overall improve my web portfolio.

The C.R.A.P design principles were a hefty part of this design process.

- Contrast is satisfied using the defined color palate.   

- Repetition is satisfied through the consistency of icons and colors used throughout. 

- Alignment is satisfied by ensuring things aren't too close or far apart from one another. 

- Proximity is satisfied by the layout of the site. The order of defining my skills, then proving it with examples and projects is logical. Grouping the projects by company/college helps define what was completed where. 


Information Architecture was also important. Having each project lead to it's own in-depth page helps define each project, while keeping things neat and tidy in other places. 


As for the structure of the site, I used a moving gif image as the first screen you see. A full screen display stating who I am and what I do looks appealing and interesting, and promotes someone to scroll further.
