UX 1400 Reflection

REFLECTION

What did I learn in this course?

In this course I learned about several tools used to create and design any UX related project.

C.R.A.P design principals were learned early on and were followed throughout the entire course. I believe this to be a "base" of UX, where these four aspects are the most basic items necessary.

Wireframes/Sitemaps were more major tools that were learned. When making this website for the first time, before this class, I hadn't done either of those things. I worked to make things look nice as I went. With these new tools, re-designing this website has helped tremendously. Preplanning ideas is much more efficient than coming up with the ideas as they are needed.

Outside of tools, Dark Design Patterns was an interesting lesson. Before learning about this, I hadn't thought about companies doing this much and associated it as normal. Learning the technical term and definition of it helps me identify where these may be applied. 


How did my ideas change about UX?

I believe that my base definition has stayed the same, but with small parts added or tweaked. I understood that the class would be about how a client experiences a product, and making the product as enjoyable and easy to use as possible.

Some things I have added to my definition are the different aspects of work that go into a UX project. Before, I had little idea how one would go about modifying or creating something that would be nice to use. C.R.A.P design principals is the main thing that I added to my definition, listing them as tools that every UX designer follows.


How does this relate to my future?

As of this year, I'm planning on declaring UX as a minor of mine at MSOE. A UX minor tied with a SE major go quite well together, as it exposes me to both a mix of front end, back end, and design development.

Having these skills will help me in future jobs or internships, as I'll be able to help in potentially several different aspects of my job.
